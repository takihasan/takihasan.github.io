Ongoing Research

  1. Environmental Sound Recognition with Spiking Neural Network Environmental sound recognition has achieving tremendous attention in past few years. Audio signal classification which includes speech and music signal classification have a traditional impact on signal classification research. Extensive research on environmental sound recognition has been expanded immensely in recent years of signal classification. This thesis focuses on several stages. Firstly, authors try to address a comparative analysis between traditional deep learning models and machine learning models on environmental sound recognition. On the other hand, another stage has been focused on developing a robust bio-inspired spiking neural network. Finally, we have compared the results with each other and build end to end solution for environmental sound recognition. In this early stage, we applied traditional convolutional neural network and residual network (ResNet-50) to the ESC-50 dataset. Where ResNet-50 shows a comprehensive result in terms of accuracy. It has achieved 85.23% accuracy. Presently, we are developing a CNN converted SNN model for environmental sound recognition. [Members: Monjur Morshed (Advisor), Taki Hasan Rafi, Sayma Saera Rahman, Tashfi Nowroz, and Nadim Khandakar]
